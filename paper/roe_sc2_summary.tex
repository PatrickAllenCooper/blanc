\begin{frame}{4 / 5 \hfill Compliance vs.\ Effectiveness Tradeoff}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\begin{columns}[T]
\begin{column}{0.52\textwidth}
\textbf{Key measurement:}\\
For each model and enforcement mode, under aggressive prompting:\\
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Proposed orders/tick}: tactical aggression proxy
  \item \textbf{Admitted orders/tick}: effective tactical output
  \item \textbf{Compliance rate}: fraction of admitted orders ROE-legal
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.4em}
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}llccc@{}}
  \toprule
  Model & Mode & Proposed & Admitted & Comply \\
  \midrule
  \multirow{2}{*}{GPT-5.4-nano} & B0 & 2.0 & 2.0 & \worst{50\%} \\
   & B2 & 2.0 & 2.0 & \best{100\%} \\
  \multirow{2}{*}{Claude S.\ 4.6} & B0 & 2.0 & 2.0 & \worst{50\%} \\
   & B2 & 2.0 & 2.0 & \best{100\%} \\
  GPT-5.2-chat & B0/B2 & 1.0 & 1.0 & 100\% \\
  \bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\normalsize
\end{column}

\begin{column}{0.45\textwidth}
\textbf{The tradeoff:}\\[0.4em]
For nano and Claude: B2 \emph{maintains} tactical output
(2.0 admitted orders/tick) while \emph{improving} compliance
(50\% $\to$ 100\%).

\vspace{0.4em}
\textbf{Why no cost?}\\
The gate blocks the violating attack (kill the protected worker)
and obtains a revised compliant attack (engage the military squad
instead). Tactical output is \emph{redirected}, not suppressed.

\vspace{0.4em}
\textbf{Implication for ROE design:}\\
In worker-protection scenarios, deploying B2 does not reduce
the unit's combat effectiveness --- it redirects it to
legitimate targets. The cost of compliance appears where
\emph{all available targets are prohibited}, which requires
a harder scenario.

\vspace{0.4em}
\note{Projection: in exclusion-zone scenarios (no alternative target),\\
B2 would reduce admitted orders to 0, incurring a real tactical cost.}
\end{column}
\end{columns}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{5 / 5 \hfill What This Establishes \& What Comes Next}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\begin{columns}[T]
\begin{column}{0.52\textwidth}
\textbf{Five established claims:}\\[0.3em]
\begin{enumerate}
  \setlength\itemsep{3pt}
  \item \textbf{Easy benchmark is saturated.}\\
        83--100\% CoT. No training signal. Ceiling confirmed.
  \item \textbf{Hard benchmark is not saturated.}\\
        \textbf{0\% CoT for all models.} Chain defeat (H1, H2)
        and multi-constraint (H4, H6) expose the capability gap.
  \item \textbf{B2 gate works: 100\% recovery.}\\
        Violations proposed under aggressive prompting are caught
        and corrected, without reducing tactical output.
  \item \textbf{Tradeoff is scenario-dependent.}\\
        Worker protection: compliance is free (redirect, not suppress).\\
        Exclusion zone: compliance has a real tactical cost.
  \item \textbf{DeepSeek/Kimi's 0-order output is an API format issue},\\
        not a capability gap --- confirmed by separate hard-seed evals.
\end{enumerate}
\end{column}
