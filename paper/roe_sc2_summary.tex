\begin{frame}{4 / 5 \hfill What This Proves (Pre-Fine-Tuning)}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

\begin{columns}[T]
\begin{column}{0.52\textwidth}
\textbf{Five supported claims:}\\[0.3em]

\begin{enumerate}
  \setlength\itemsep{4pt}
  \item \textbf{ROE formalizes correctly.}\\
        KB certified; all 6 easy + 4 hard seeds pass the formal triple.

  \item \textbf{Easy benchmark is saturated; hard benchmark is not.}\\
        Easy CoT: 33--100\% per model.
        Hard CoT: \textbf{0\% for every model.}
        The gap is the training target.

  \item \textbf{H4 refuted: ROE syntax is harder, not easier.}\\
        L3 E1 (decoder failure) rate = 61.8\%, higher than L2 (38.1\%).

  \item \textbf{B2 gate recovery is 100\%.}\\
        Under adversarial prompt, violations are proposed and caught.
        Gate never admitted a violating order.

  \item \textbf{Object-level protection is structurally weaker.}\\
        Models respect spatial ROE without prompting.
        Worker protection breaks under pressure.
\end{enumerate}
\end{column}

\begin{column}{0.45\textwidth}
\textbf{What the hard benchmark adds:}\\[0.4em]

The easy seeds are useful for \emph{profiling} models
(which ones understand ROE at all).
The hard seeds are useful for \emph{measuring improvement}
(did fine-tuning help?).

\vspace{0.5em}
\textbf{Hard seed properties:}\\[0.2em]
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
  H1 & Chain defeat (2 layers) \\
  H2 & 3-step reasoning chain \\
  H4 & 3 competing constraints \\
  H6 & Multi-anomaly conservativity \\
\end{tabular}
\normalsize

\vspace{0.5em}
These map directly to the \textbf{DeFAb-Hard} pre-registration
axes from \textit{paper.tex}: high novelty, scaled depth,
multi-anomaly conservativity.

\vspace{0.4em}
\textbf{Cost:} full easy + hard evaluation, all 5 models = \textbf{\$4.50 total.}
\end{column}
\end{columns}

\begin{columns}[T]
\begin{column}{0.50\textwidth}
\textbf{Three implications for the project:}\\[0.4em]

\textbf{1. The verifier is a working behavioral guardrail.}\\
The polynomial-time engine gates LLM decisions against
formal policy at inference time --- 100\% recovery rate.

\vspace{0.4em}
\textbf{2. ROE provides a two-tier evaluation surface.}\\
Easy tier: profile models pre-training.\\
Hard tier: measure improvement post-training.\\
Together they bound the capability gap that fine-tuning must close.

\vspace{0.4em}
\textbf{3. The game proxy generalizes.}\\
The defeasible framework applies to any structured policy domain
--- real ROE, clinical protocols, autonomous systems.
\end{column}
